Tournament Information

Registration opens on Tuesday September 19, 2017, at 12:30 PM ET. This year, we are happy to announce that the participation fee will be waived! We believe that Science Olympiad tournaments should be financially accessible to all, and we hope that waiving the participation fee will allow teams to attend more invitational tournaments across the country. The registration procedures detailed below are subject to change.

Registration

  • All schools must be a registered member of a state Science Olympiad organization in good standing.
  • Limit of 48 teams on a first-register first-serve basis.
  • The first 12 schools to successfully register two teams will receive two slots each in the tournament. Subsequent schools to successfully register will receive one slot each in the tournament.
  • Teams will be placed on the waitlist in chronological order after the registration limit has been reached.
  • Head coaches will receive email confirmation upon submitting the registration form. If confirmation has not been receieved within 48 hours, please email us at scioly@princeton.edu.
  • While there is no participation fee this year, schools are required to mail a deposit of $75 per team made out to "Princeton University Science Olympiad" by Thursday October 26, 2017 in order to finalize its alloted slots in the tournament. Thus, a school confirmed for one team should mail a deposit of $75, while a school confirmed for two teams should mail a deposit of $150.
    • We will deposit a check only if a school withdraws one or both of its teams from the tournament after Friday December 1, 2017. Checks will not be deposited (no fee incurred) otherwise.
    • This deposit system is meant to ensure that teams admitted off the waitlist have adequate time to plan for attending our tournament. It is also meant to enforce commitment to attending our tournament.
  • Please mail all checks to the below address:

Competition Policies

  • All events will be run by Science Olympiad alumni who have medaled at Nationals or the State level, and all tests/labs will be revised by our executive board for consistency.
  • All tests and keys with raw scores will be made available to teams after competition.
  • Every team will be assigned a homeroom to stay in during competition day.
  • Each team must be accompanied by one unique adult. Schools with two teams thus must be accompanied by two unique adults.
  • The tournament schedule will adhere closely to the national tournament schedule.
  • All events will follow the national Science Olympiad Inc. rules as well as clarifications posted at soinc.org/events/rules-clarifications. For events that have different rules or content for different levels of competition, we will adhere to the rules specified at the regional level.
  • Competitors must adhere to the code of ethics and general policies outlined at soinc.org/join/policies.
  • Competitors, parents and coaches may photograph or record videos of their own team's build devices but may not do so for another team without permission. We will have staff photographers take photos throughout the day that will be posted post-tournament.
